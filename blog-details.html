The Secret to the Perfect Funnel Cake Batter

Marcus Chen
| May 12, 2026 | 5 min read

For years, customers have asked us: "How do you get it so crispy on the outside but perfectly fluffy on the inside?" Today, we're pulling back the curtain on the Sugar Cloud funnel cake method.

It's All in the Temperature

The biggest mistake home cooks make when trying to replicate carnival funnel cake is oil temperature. If your oil is too cold, the batter absorbs it and becomes greasy. Too hot, and the outside burns before the inside cooks. We keep our fryers exactly at 375°F (190°C).

"A funnel cake should taste like a sweet memory, not a deep fryer." - Marcus Chen

The Secret Ingredient

Most standard recipes call for milk and water. We substitute half the liquid with a club soda. The carbonation adds tiny air pockets to the batter, creating a lighter, crispier texture that holds up perfectly under heavy toppings like our signature salted caramel.

Basic Recipe Ratio

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 cup club soda (cold)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Mix the dry ingredients separately. Whisk the wet ingredients, then gently fold them into the dry. Do not overmix! Lumps are okay. Pour through a funnel (hence the name) in a swirling motion into your hot oil. Fry for 1 minute per side until golden brown.
